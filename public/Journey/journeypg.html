School Journey

I completed my 10th standard in the academic year 2022–23 from Oxford School with an impressive score of 9.2 CGPA out of 10. From nursery to 10th class, I studied in the same school, which gave me countless memories and experiences that I will always cherish. I used to travel to school by bus, and those journeys were one of the most enjoyable parts of my school life. My friends and I would play games while going to school and continue them again on the way back home. I especially loved P.T.E and computer periods because our teachers would take us to the computer lab and teach us the basics of computers such as the monitor, keyboard, mouse, and software like PowerPoint and MS Office. After completing our tasks, we would secretly play games on the computers too 😁. Sports period was another exciting part of my school days. We used to play badminton, kho-kho, basketball, and also do exercises guided by our teachers. Basketball has always been my favorite sport. One of the most unforgettable moments of my school life happened during my 5th standard when I scored 18 perfect basketball shots consecutively. Everyone was shocked, and our principal, Jafar Matheen, praised me and said, “You can become a great basketball player.” That compliment from my principal made me incredibly happy and motivated. Although Telugu was the subject I struggled with the most and where I lost many marks, I am proud that I worked very hard to improve myself. I used to wake up at 4 A.M. to memorize poems and kavi parichayams, which also made my mornings productive and disciplined. My 10th class journey was truly special because I created many wonderful memories and became very close to all my teachers, as it was the final year they would teach us. Those school days will always remain one of the happiest chapters of my life.

Intermediate Journey

I completed my Intermediate in the academic years 2023–25 from Sri Chaitanya Junior College with an impressive score of 923 out of 1000. During my first year, I made many good friends, and we used to help each other by explaining topics and discussing concepts together. At the same time, we also had healthy competition among ourselves and with students from other campuses through regular exams. Intermediate life showed me the real meaning of competition, where students worked extremely hard with dreams of getting into top IITs such as Indian Institute of Technology Bombay, Indian Institute of Technology Delhi, and Indian Institute of Technology Madras. One of the most memorable moments of my Intermediate life was the fest conducted during our second year, which was also our final year in college. During the fest, I met many of my old school friends again, and we enjoyed the event a lot. However, I also felt sad because some of my best friends from the first year changed their colleges in the second year. Intermediate life introduced me to many different kinds of people with unique mindsets and personalities, which helped me understand people better and grow as a person. On the last day of college, we clicked many pictures together and left the campus carrying countless unforgettable memories with us.

This journey is helping me grow both academically and technically, and it motivates me to keep learning new concepts in the field of software development and data science.
